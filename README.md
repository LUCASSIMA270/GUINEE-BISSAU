# GUINEE-BISSAU

🌍 Bissau Circular: Community-Driven Waste Management
Empowering Neighborhoods for a Cleaner and Healthier Bissau

This repository hosts the strategic framework, logical models, and activity plans for the Team 3 initiative aimed at improving solid waste management through community participation and circular economy principles.
🧐 The Mission

The overall objective is to improve sustainable solid waste management in Bissau by promoting recycling and collective initiatives. The project specifically aims to:

    Promote Circularity: Increase the adoption of circular economy and recycling practices.

    Capacity Building: Enhance the organizational and technical abilities of stakeholders.

    Citizen Engagement: Mobilize the population to actively maintain a cleaner city.

💡 Our Innovative Strategy: The "Bairro Captain" Model

At the heart of Team 3’s proposal is a decentralized waste management network.

    Neighborhood Leaders: Each block ("bairro") designates a Bairro Captain responsible for waste collection coordination and awareness.

    Collaborative Governance: The network operates alongside the Camara Municipal de Bissau to bridge the gap between citizens and decision-makers.

    Dynamic Response: Citizens use a reporting system to photograph illegal dumps, enabling rapid removal.

🛠️ Project Outputs & Key Results

The project is structured around five core results:

    Urban Attractiveness & Health: Increasing tourism and business appeal while reducing pollution-related illnesses by 15%.

    Infrastructure Development: A 50% increase in recycling handling capacity through new and rehabilitated sites.

    Structured Coordination: Establishing formal liaison between neighborhood representatives and city officials.

    Environmental Literacy: Integrating recycling and circular economy education into school curricula and community workshops.

    Professional Inclusion: Formalizing the status of informal waste pickers, aiming for a 90% reduction in unregulated picking.

📈 Expected Impact

    30% Reduction in waste ending up in open dumpsites within 5 years.

    50% Increase in total municipal waste diverted to recycling.

    Improved Quality of Life: Measured through annual household perception surveys regarding neighborhood cleanliness.
